Cheese Cake



Cheesecake is a very popular dessert which many people enjoy. It has a rich creamy texture and comes in many flavors. It usually is served with whipping cream, and it can be topped with fruit or other toppings.

Ingredients

    Graham Cracker Crust

  • 1 and ½ cups graham cracker crumbs (about 12 full sheet crackers)
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted

    • Cheesecake

  • 32 ounces full-fat cream cheese, softened to room temperature
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup full fat sour cream, at room temperature. If you don’t have sour cream, you can use greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoon fresh lemon juice (optional, but recommended)
  • 3 large eggs, at room temperature

Recipe Instructions

    How to Make

    Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 350OF

  2. If you are starting with full graham crackers, use a food processor to blender to grind them into crumbs

  3. Pour the crumbs and mix it with sugar until combined

  4. Mix the sugar and crumbs with the melted butter. Try to mash or break up any large chunks or lumps.

  5. Pour into an ungreased 9 or 10 inch pan.

  6. Pat the crumbs down into the bottom and partly up the sides to make a compact crust. Do not pat too hard otherwise the crust will be too hard. Pat it down till the mixture is no longer crumbly. You can use the bottom of a measuring cup to flatten it out more.

    7. Filling

  7. Use a handheld or stand mixer to beat the cream cheese and granulated sugar together on medium high speed in a large bowl until the mixture is smooth and creamy. This may take about two minutes.

  8. Add the sour cream/greek yogurt, vanilla extract, and lemon juice then beat til fully combined

  9. On medium speed, add the eggs one at a time, beating after each addition until blended. After the final egg is mixed into the batter, stop mixing.

  10. To help prevent the cheesecake from deflating and cracking as it cools, avoid over mixing the batter as best as you can. You will have close to 6 cups of batter.

    11. Water Bath

  11. Fill a kettle with water and boil it. As the water is heating up, wrap aluminum foil around the crust pan

  12. Place the pan with the crust inside a larger pan

  13. Pour the batter on top of the crust. Use a rubber spatula of spoon to smooth it into an even layer

  14. Carefully pour one inch of the boiled water into the larger pan, then place the large pan into the oven. (note: the pan with the water isn’t the same as the one with the actual cheesecake)

  15. Place the smaller pan with the cheesecake carefully into the larger pan. Both pans should be in the oven

  16. Bake the cheesecake for 55-70 minutes or until the center is almost set. If you notice the cheesecake browning too quickly on the top, cover it with a foil halfway through baking

  17. When it's done, the center will slightly wobble if you gently shake the pan

  18. Turn the oven off and open the oven door slightly. Let the cheesecake sit in the oven as it cools down fo an hour

  19. Cover and refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours are overnight

  20. Use a knife to cut the cheesecake into slices

  21. Serve with the toppings of your choice. Enjoy!
