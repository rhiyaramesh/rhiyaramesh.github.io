Brownies

About the Dish

Brownies are a sweet similar to choclate cake. It is made with a chocolate batter and can contain chocolate chips and even some nuts. Brownies are usually shaped in squares or rectangles, and can be eaten with ice cream.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups of granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cups of all purpose flour
  • 2/3 cups of cocoa powder (sifted if lumpy)
  • 1/2 cups of powdered sugar (sifted if lumpy)
  • 1/2 cups of dark chocolate chips
  • 3/4 teaspoons of sea salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cups of canola oil (or extra virgin olive oil)
  • 2 tablespoons of water
  • 1/2 teaspoons of vanilla

Recipe Instructions

    How to Make

  1. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees fahrenheit. Lightly spray an 8x8 baking dish (not a 9x9 dish, or else the brownies will overcook) with cooking spray and line it with parchment paper.

  2. In a medium bowl, combine the sugar, flour, cocoa powder, chocolate chips, and salt.

  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, olive oil, water, and vanilla.

  4. Sprinkle the dry mix over the wet mix and stir until just combined.

  5. Pour the batter into the prepared pan (it will be thick - that's ok) and use a spatula to smooth the top.

  6. Bake for 40-48 minutes, or until a toothpick that was inserted comes out with only a few crumbs attached (Note: it's better to pull the brownies out early than leave them in too long).

  7. Cool completely before slicing. Store in an airtight container room temperature for up to three days. These brownies also freeze well. You can eat them with a side of ice cream, if desired. Enjoy!
