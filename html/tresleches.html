Tresleches



Tres Leches is a cake, made of of three types of milk, which is why it's called "tres leches" or "3 milks" It is a sponge cake soaked in milk and topped with fresh whipped cream and cinnamon. Sometimes, it can also contain caramel, and can be topped with fresh strawberries.

Ingredients

    Cake

  • 1 cup of all purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups of baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoons of salt
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 cup of granulated eggs
  • 1/3 cups of whole milk (any kind of milk with the same amount)
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

    • Milk Mixture

  • 12 ounce can of evaporated milk
  • 14 ounce can of sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup of whole milk (any kind of milk with the same amount)

    • Whipped Topping

  • 1 pint of heavy whipping cream
  • 3 tablespoons of powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon (for topping)

Recipe Instructions

    How to Make

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees fahrenheit.

  2. In a medium bowl, mix flour, baking powder, and salt. Separate the eggs into two other mixing bowls. One for the egg yolks and one for the egg whites.

  3. Add 3/4 cups of sugar to the bowl with the egg yolks and mix on high speed till the yolks are pale yellow. Add 1/3 cups of milk and vanilla and stir. Pour the egg yolk mixture over the flour mixture and gently mix till the ingredients and combines. Don't over mix.

  4. Use electric beaters to beat the egg whites at high speed. As they begin to whip into stiff peaks, gradually mix in the remaining 1/4 cups of sugar. Fold the stiffly beaten egg whites into the batter gently, scraping the bottom and sides of the bowl, till it's combined.

  5. Pour the batter into an ungreased pan and smoothen it out into an even layer.

  6. Bake for 25-35 minutes, or until a toothpick that was inserted in the center of the cake comes out clean. Once ready, remove from the oven and allow the cake to cool completely.

  7. Combine the evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and whole milk in a bowl. Once the cake has cooled, use a fork to poke holes all over the top of the cake.

  8. Slowly pour the milk mixture over the top of the cake. Make sure to pour near the edges and all around.

  9. Refridgirate for at least an hour. to allow it to soak up all the milk.

  10. In the meantime, whip the heavy cream, sugar, and vanilla until stiff peaks. Smooth the paste over the top of the cake.

  11. To garnish, sprinkle cinnamon on top. Serve with fresh, sliced strawberries on the top or on the side if desired. Enjoy!
